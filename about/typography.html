This Is a Test Page With HTML Text Used In Different Scenarios

This page is for testing our theme’s style code (CSS). It starts with simple examples of the H1—H6 heading elements followed by headings used in other ways.

Headings should provide a scannable outline of the page. Our readers should be able to skim the headings to find the most important information on the page. For SEO and usability, headings should use plain language. They should use words and phrases that our readers use when searching for our content.

Single Line Heading Examples

Heading Level One, The Page Title

For accessibility, the page title should be the only H1 on the page.

Heading Level Two, First H2 After A Page Title

The first heading in the content area of the page should be H2. The first heading two after the page title has a smaller top margin.

Heading Level Two

It’s common for a page to have multiple H2s. They should form a scannable outline of the main topics on the page. A heading two often has a larger top margin to separate the main topics of the page.

Heading Level Three

A heading three is a subtopic of heading two.

Heading Level Four

A heading four is a subtopic of heading three.

Heading Level Five

A heading five is uncommon. I’ve not seen a use-case for H5 on any OIT sites so far.

Heading Level Six

A heading six is very uncommon. I have not needed to use a heading six on an academic website in my career.
